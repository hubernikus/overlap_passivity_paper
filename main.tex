\begin{abstract}
Real-time velocity evaluation for control of robots increases the system's resilience against noise and disturbances. The desired velocities can be designed to ensure dynamic obstacle avoidance. The desired velocity is often coupled with a passive torque controller to ensure safe responses to external perturbations. 
We propose a novel method for designing the passive control policy; the proposed approach internalizes obstacles to ensure collision avoidance while handling external forces.
Our method exhibits compliant behavior away from obstacles but increases damping when approaching obstacles. The algorithm ensures stability across all scenarios, effectively countering disturbances.
The effectiveness of the approach is validated using a 7DoF robot arm. The algorithm demonstrates superior collision rejection compared to the baseline, underscoring its usefulness for real-world applications. Our proposed obstacle-aware passivity controller represents a significant step towards safe robot control in complex and uncertain environments.
\end{abstract}
